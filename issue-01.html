Issue 01 / May 12, 2026

A field guide to noticing strange useful things.

This first issue defines the new WeirdWeek rule: a subject is worth covering when its oddness reveals a real constraint, tradeoff, or human habit.

Odd is not enough

A thing can look strange and still be a thin topic. We look for the deeper question: what problem made this form reasonable to someone?

Systems beat lists

The archive is about systems, design history, infrastructure, and useful explanations.

Sources matter

Each article includes source notes or reading context. The goal is not to pretend every short explainer is a research paper, but to make claims checkable.

Readers first

Pages should answer a real curiosity. If a reader leaves only knowing that something exists, the article is not finished.
