Editorial Standards

About WeirdWeek

An independent editorial project that finds, reviews, and contextualizes unusual consumer products — no storefront, no affiliate links, no empty clickbait.

What WeirdWeek Is

WeirdWeek.fun is a human-curated editorial website about unusual marketplace products and online shopping culture. Every item we cover gets original commentary: what it is, why people notice it, where the joke ends, and what a buyer should think about before taking it seriously.

We do not sell products, process payments, collect orders, warehouse inventory, dropship, or represent any marketplace. We are not a store. We are an editorial team writing about objects that make the internet feel handmade, chaotic, and oddly creative.

How We Select Products

Items are selected by hand from public marketplace listings. We use a consistent lens to evaluate each find:

  • Conversation value — does the object create a reaction beyond "that's weird"?
  • Practical value — is there any genuine use, or is it purely a prop?
  • Safety and comfort — does the novelty create a real risk or discomfort?
  • Originality — is this a creative design, or just a copy of something we've already covered?
  • Honesty — does the listing exaggerate, or does the product deliver what it promises?

What We Exclude

We do not publish products where the joke depends on harm, harassment, counterfeit claims, or adult content. We exclude duplicate or near-duplicate items, listings with misleading pricing, and products with overwhelmingly negative safety reports.

Who Runs This

WeirdWeek is written and maintained by an independent editor with a background in retail operations and AI. The project started as a way to document the strange, creative, and occasionally brilliant corners of online marketplaces — and grew into a weekly publication.

The site is funded by its editor. Advertising, when present, is managed through Google AdSense and is never placed on empty pages, navigation screens, or policy pages.
