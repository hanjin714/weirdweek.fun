Systems that look absurd until the job becomes obvious.

This issue is about forms that seem strange from the outside: pipes for mail, ships painted in broken stripes, and round metal covers in streets. Each one becomes less odd when the constraint is visible.

Everyday systems

Why manhole covers are usually round

A familiar street object that hides safety, orientation, manufacturing, and maintenance logic.

Editorial note

WeirdWeek now treats oddness as a starting point, not the whole value of a page. If a topic cannot explain a real tradeoff, system, history, or design constraint, it does not belong in the archive.
